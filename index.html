Synthesis of N-Methyl-1-Phenylpropan-2-Amine from Toluene: A Comprehensive Theoretical Study for Educational Laboratories

Author: Anonymous
Date: July 2025

Abstract

This paper presents a theoretical synthesis of N-methyl-1-phenylpropan-2-amine (C10H15N) from toluene, designed for educational laboratories using standard reagents and equipment. The four-step procedure involves benzylic chlorination with trichloroisocyanuric acid (TCCA), a Grignard reaction to form phenylacetic acid, ketonic decarboxylation to produce phenylacetone, and reductive amination with methylamine and sodium borohydride to yield the target amine as its hydrochloride salt. Reagents such as toluene, TCCA, magnesium, ethanol, dry ice, acetic anhydride, methylamine hydrochloride, and sodium borohydride are selected for accessibility.
