Appendix A: Safety Data Sheets Summary

  • Toluene: Flammable liquid (flash point 4°C), causes skin and eye irritation, harmful if inhaled. Store in a cool, ventilated area.
  • TCCA: Strong oxidizer, releases chlorine gas, corrosive to skin and eyes. Handle in a fume hood with protective equipment.
  • Benzoyl Peroxide: Explosive when dry, irritant to skin and eyes. Store in small quantities away from heat sources.
  • THF: Flammable liquid (flash point -14°C), may form explosive peroxides, irritant to skin and eyes. Store under nitrogen in a cool, ventilated area.
  • Methylamine: Flammable gas, toxic by inhalation, causes burns. Generate and use in a fume hood.
  • NaBH4: Reacts with water to release hydrogen, irritant to skin and eyes. Add slowly to avoid gas evolution.

Appendix B: TLC Monitoring Protocols

TLC was performed using silica gel plates with hexane:ethyl acetate (9:1 for chlorination, 7:3 for reductive amination). Spots were visualized under UV light (254 nm). Typical Rf values:

  • Toluene: 0.8 (9:1).
  • Benzyl Chloride: 0.6 (9:1).
  • Phenylacetone: 0.5 (7:3).
  • N-Methyl-1-Phenylpropan-2-Amine: 0.3 (7:3).

Plates must be freshly prepared, and solvent systems optimized to ensure clear separation.

Appendix C: Regulatory Guidelines

Under the NDPS Act, synthesis of N-methyl-1-phenylpropan-2-amine requires:

  • An NCB permit specifying the academic purpose.
  • Detailed records of reagent quantities and usage.
  • Secure storage of intermediates (e.g., phenylacetone) in locked cabinets.
  • Disposal through authorized chemical waste facilities.

Permit applications must be submitted 60 days in advance, with compliance logs maintained.

Appendix D: Sample Yield Calculations

  • Step 1: Toluene (12.8 mL, 0.12 mol, MW 92.14 g/mol) yields benzyl chloride (7.6–10.6 g, 0.06–0.084 mol, MW 126.58 g/mol, 50–70%).
  • Step 2: Benzyl chloride (10.6 g, 0.084 mol) in THF (80 mL) yields phenylacetic acid (6.8–8.6 g, 0.0504–0.063 mol, MW 136.15 g/mol, 60–75%).
  • Step 3: Phenylacetic acid (8.6 g, 0.063 mol) yields phenylacetone (5.1–5.9 g, 0.0378–0.0441 mol, MW 134.18 g/mol, 60–70%).
  • Step 4: Phenylacetone (5.9 g, 0.044 mol) yields N-methyl-1-phenylpropan-2-amine hydrochloride (5.7–6.5 g, 0.0308–0.0352 mol, MW 185.69 g/mol, 70–80%).

Overall yield: 0.0308 mol × 185.69 g/mol = 5.7 g (minimum).

Appendix E: Safety Checklist

  • Pre-Lab: Verify fume hood functionality, prepare spill kits, and ensure eyewash stations are accessible.
  • During Lab: Wear nitrile gloves, safety goggles, and lab coats. Handle TCCA, benzyl chloride, THF, and methylamine in a fume hood.
  • Post-Lab: Neutralize acidic waste, collect organic waste in designated containers, and dispose of through authorized facilities.
